WHAT are E-WASTE?

E-waste are electronic products that are unwanted, not working, and nearing or at the end of their "useful life". The Philippines is deemed a leading e-waste producer in Southeast Asia, mainly from consumer electronics like TVs, refrigirators, and mobile phones

(DW team, 2024) "Philippines faces escalating e-waste crisis"
Photo by UNIDO.org

WHAT are the causes of E-WASTE?

Higher consumption of electric and electronic equipment, as well as short lifecycles and limited repair options are what's driving the increase in electronic waste generated worldwide

(UN Industrial Development Organization, 2022) "The Philippines: making money making e-waste safe"
Photo by yira.org

WHAT are the effects of E-WASTE?

The Philippines is a haven for a wide variety of aquatic life, including coral reefs, mangrove forests, and endangered species; improper e-waste disposal directly endagers these delicate ecosystems and results in irreparable damage and biodiversity loss. The deterioration of Manila Bay, a cornerstone of the country's capital, underscores this issues

(Ong, A., 2023) "A Technological Travesty: E-waste in the Philippines"
Photo by philstar.com

WHAT is the current status of E-WASTE in the Philippines?

"E-waste is a rapidly growing waste stream in the country. From 3.9 kilos per capita in 2019, 537 million kilograms of e-waste were produced in the Philippines, translating to 4.7 kilograms of e-waste per capita in 2022," said Jam Lorenzo, research and policy development officer of BAN Toxics, an environmental justice NGO

(Cariaso, B., 2024) "Philippines among top e-waste producers in SEA"

Answering HOW can we help?

Photo by pia.gov

EXTENDED PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY

In a bid to tackle the mammoth plastic waste problem in the Philippines, the Republic Act (RA) No. 11898 — otherwise known as the Extended Producer Responsibility Act of 2022 — lapsed into law on the 23rd July 2022. In a nutshell, the law holds companies accountable for the plastic packaging they produce throughout the lifecycle of their products. Before this law was introduced, manufacturers were only responsible for the impact of the production of their products, rather than the whole lifecycle. However, they now need to account for all packaging used to protect, transport, and sell the product. This means the legislation impacts both up and downstream business activities. Unlike many EPR systems around the world, the Philippine law includes the recovery of flexible plastic, which has been historically difficult (and sometimes impossible) to sell. This is a game changer for the environment, as it incentivizes people to recover non-recyclable waste.

(Howell, B., 2023) "How Does Extended Producer Responsibility Work in the Philippines?"
Photo by factasia.org

COMMUNITY RECYCLING INITIATIVES

The Philippines is making significant progress towards addressing its waste management challenges through recycling programs. A combination of government interventions, community-driven initiatives, and private sector engagement allows the implementation of policies and the emergence of recycling programs at the grassroots level.

(Cubacub, N., 2024) "Charting the Progress of Recycling Programs in the Philippines"
Photo by ecowastecoalition.blogspot.com

E-WASTE COLLECTION EVENTS

The five-day “Simultaneous E-Waste Collection” event of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources- Environmental Management Bureau (DENR-EMB) and the United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO), in cooperation with the EcoWaste Coalition, concluded last June 30, 2023. The event was held in collaboration with the DENR-EMB NCR Regional Office and local government units of Baguio City, Manila City, and the City of San Jose del Monte, along with DMCI, Megaworld, Globe Telecom, PLDT/Smart Telecom, and the SyCip Law Center.

(EcoWaste Coalition, 2023) "CULMINATION OF THE SIMULTANEOUS E-WASTE COLLECTION EVENT: PARTNERSHIP LEADS THE WAY TOWARD SAFE E-WASTE MANAGEMENT"
