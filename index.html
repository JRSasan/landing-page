WHAT are E-WASTE?

E-waste are electronic products that are unwanted, not working, and nearing or at the end of their "useful life". The Philippines is deemed a leading e-waste producer in Southeast Asia, mainly from consumer electronics like TVs, refrigirators, and mobile phones

(DW team, 2024) "Philippines faces escalating e-waste crisis"
Photo by UNIDO.org

WHAT are the causes of E-WASTE?

Higher consumption of electric and electronic equipment, as well as short lifecycles and limited repair options are what's driving the increase in electronic waste generated worldwide

(UN Industrial Development Organization, 2022) "The Philippines: making money making e-waste safe"
Photo by yira.org

WHAT are the effects of E-WASTE?

The Philippines is a haven for a wide variety of aquatic life, including coral reefs, mangrove forests, and endangered species; improper e-waste disposal directly endagers these delicate ecosystems and results in irreparable damage and biodiversity loss. The deterioration of Manila Bay, a cornerstone of the country's capital, underscores this issues

(Ong, A., 2023) "A Technological Travesty: E-waste in the Philippines"
Photo by yira.org

WHAT is the current status of E-WASTE in the Philippines?

"E-waste is a rapidly growing waste stream in the country. From 3.9 kilos per capita in 2019, 537 million kilograms of e-waste were produced in the Philippines, translating to 4.7 kilograms of e-waste per capita in 2022," said Jam Lorenzo, research and policy development officer of BAN Toxics, an environmental justice NGO

(Cariaso, B., 2024) "Philippines among top e-waste producers in SEA"
